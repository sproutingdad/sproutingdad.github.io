Dirt Relics

Dirt Relics is an ongoing ceramic practice exploring the relationship between earth and memory.

I began making ceramics in 2022 to fulfill my longstanding curiosity about the medium. As I learned about the prehistoric origins of pottery and the powerful elemental forces that render organic material into non-biodegradable vitrified solids, I began to consider more deeply the environmental and historical impact of my works. The instinct to produce completed works for the sake of accumulation no longer resonated with me. The fundamental nature of practice in pottery is the ability to make and remake forms with minimal resource usage. I have always loved how clay can be infinitely recycled and endlessly reworked until it is fired. I believe the practice of making pottery can teach us to release attachment to objects or concepts that we do not find personal meaning in, reiterate as a process for clarification, and appreciate the unpredictable beauty wrought by extreme natural forces. The name “Dirt Relics” signifies the transformation of a conventional, often deemed “lesser”, organic matter into a crystallized form that will one day outlive us all.
